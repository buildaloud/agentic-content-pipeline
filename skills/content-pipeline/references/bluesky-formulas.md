# Bluesky Formulas

Named shapes for a Bluesky post. The outline step picks one per post
(`postFormula` in the meta block, drawn from this file instead of
`post-formulas.md` when the medium is `bluesky`); `outline-structure-reviewer`
(outline grain) and `impact-reviewer` (draft grain) check the shape actually
holds. Same craft bar as the blog army — no AI tells; `bluesky-reviewer` and
the tone gate both run on these drafts.

Pick by what the post *is*, not by habit.

---

## single-shot
One idea, stated flat and complete, in a single post.
- **The idea** — the whole thing, in ≤300 characters. No cliffhanger, no "more
  below" — this post stands alone.
- **Voice** — confident and flat. State it; don't hedge or soften it into a
  question unless the point genuinely is a question.
- Best when: a real opinion or observation that doesn't need a story to land.

## thread
A hook post that fully stands alone, followed by 2-6 self-contained beats.
- **The hook post** — one complete idea in ≤300 characters, readable and
  satisfying on its own even if nobody continues the thread. Never a teaser
  that only makes sense once you know a thread follows.
- **The beats** — each reply is its own self-contained thought (≤300
  characters), building the case or story one step at a time. No beat should
  require the next one to make sense in isolation.
- **No engagement-bait framing.** Never open with "🧵 1/n" or "a thread you
  need to read" — let the hook post's own content signal there's more, if
  there's more worth reading.
- **Number only if the sequence matters.** If the beats are a genuine ordered
  sequence (steps, a timeline), numbering earns its place. If they're just
  parallel points, skip numbers.
- Best when: a real multi-beat story or argument where each beat carries its
  own weight.

## link-share
One sharp line plus a URL.
- **The line** — a real take or angle on what's behind the link, not a bare
  "check this out" or a restatement of the linked title.
- **The link** — Bluesky renders link cards and does **not** depress reach for
  posts with links (the opposite of LinkedIn's contested-folklore rule — see
  `references/linkedin-formulas.md`). Link freely; no placement workaround
  needed.
- Best when: something worth pointing at exists and doesn't need a full post
  of original commentary.

---

## Rules for every formula
- **300 characters is a hard limit per post**, counted in graphemes (emoji and
  multi-byte characters count as one character each, not by byte length).
- One idea per post (or per thread beat). Bluesky rewards a clear, complete
  thought over a compressed essay.
- Formula is a skeleton, not a cage — break a beat when the material demands
  it, but know you're breaking it.

## Format facts (hard rules, not style choices)
- **300-character hard limit, per post, counted in graphemes.** Not
  approximate — a post over the limit doesn't publish. Count before writing,
  not after.
- **No markdown rendering.** Bluesky shows literal `**`/`#`/`-` characters, not
  bold/headers/bullets. Write plain text.
- **Links are fine, unlike LinkedIn.** Bluesky renders a link card
  automatically and does not depress reach for link posts — there is no
  comment-placement workaround to reach for here.
- **Mentions and hashtags exist but the culture is light-touch.** A `@handle`
  or one relevant hashtag is normal; hashtag-stacking or mention-spam reads as
  spam here same as anywhere.

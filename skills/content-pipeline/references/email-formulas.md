# Email Formulas

Named shapes for an email post/newsletter. The outline step picks one per post
(`postFormula` in the meta block, drawn from this file instead of
`post-formulas.md` when the medium is `email`); `outline-structure-reviewer`
(outline grain) and `impact-reviewer` (draft grain) check the shape actually
holds. Same craft bar as the blog army — no AI tells; `email-reviewer` and the
tone gate both run on these drafts.

Pick by what the email *is*, not by habit.

---

## announcement
Something shipped or changed; tell the reader plainly, then give them the one
thing to do about it.
- **Subject + preheader** — name the thing, concretely (see Format facts for caps).
- **The news** — what changed, in the first sentence. No warm-up.
- **Why it matters to the reader** — one paragraph, their angle, not yours.
- **The one CTA** — a single, specific action.
- Best when: a real change worth a dedicated email, not a minor tweak.

## story-digest
A short story or update, in the same honest voice as the blog, compressed to
email length.
- **Subject + preheader** — the hook, compressed to fit the caps.
- **The story** — 2-3 short paragraphs, one thread, no sub-plots.
- **The takeaway** — one honest line.
- **The one CTA** — read the full post, reply, or whatever the single ask is.
- Best when: a blog-post-shaped story that's better as a short, personal note
  than a full article.

## single-idea-essay
One idea, argued briefly, no filler.
- **Subject + preheader** — state the idea's angle, not a vague tease.
- **The idea** — stated flat in the first paragraph.
- **The case** — 1-2 paragraphs of real support (evidence, example, number).
- **The one CTA** — a single, specific next step.
- Best when: a real opinion worth a reader's two minutes, not a full post.

---

## Rules for every formula
- One CTA per email. Two asks means the reader does neither.
- Plain, conversational text — write like a note to one person, not a
  broadcast.
- No image-dependent content — if the email is unreadable with images off (the
  common default), it's broken. Never put essential text inside an image.
- Formula is a skeleton, not a cage — break a beat when the material demands
  it, but know you're breaking it.

## Format facts (hard rules, not style choices)
- **Subject line: ≤50 characters.** Concrete and specific, never clickbait
  ("the thing that broke our build" not "you won't believe what happened").
- **Preheader: ≤90 characters.** Complements the subject — extends the promise,
  never just repeats it verbatim.
- **One CTA.** State it once, clearly; don't bury a second ask lower in the body.
- **Deliverability tells to avoid:** ALL CAPS words, exclamation-point stacking
  ("free!!!"), the word "free" paired with urgency language, excessive links
  (more than 2-3 in a short email reads spammy to filters and readers alike).
- **Scannable body.** Short paragraphs, no dense walls of text — most email
  clients render on a phone by default.
